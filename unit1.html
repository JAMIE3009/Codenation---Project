1.4 - Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?


Risks associated with digital projects can be ten-fold, I shall endeavour to limit these risks to 5;
1. Misalignment with business processes – How and why are you doing this idea? How is it being advertised? Have you and your team/company all agreed on the end products vision? It is all well and good having the best marketting team and or idea, but, you must not overlook the resulting impacts on the company’s business processes, the initiative is likely to fail if you do this. 2. A Lack of cross- functional expertise – One of the biggest misconceptions in the digital process is the need for ‘techy people’ only on the project, this could not be further away from the truth and the team would massively benefit from a wide variety of expertise. 3. Limited digital strategy – This one is more centred towards the strategy of the project, and how it is important to define how success would look for the project and how that contributes to the companies and or individuals business strategy. 4. Fixed plans - to the point they do not account for change. This one kind of writes itself, but put simply, one must be flexible and open to change. Technology is an ever changing landscape, you must be able to adapt for your projects’ success. 5. Over ambition - This one may sound a little strange, but when you consider the endless possibilities that tech provides and then account in the realistic elements of business craft / human ability , it is important to manage expectations and have a viable product as the starting point, opposed to attempting to reach AAA standards when they might not be attainable as a starting point

1.7 - Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?


General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a legal framework that sets guidelines for the collection and processing of personal information from individuals who live in the European Union (EU). • Lawfulness, fairness and transparency. • Purpose limitation. • Data minimisation. • Accuracy. • Storage limitation. • Integrity and confidentiality (security) • Accountability. GDPR

The Internet has dramatically changed the way we communicate and how we handle everyday tasks. We share documents, we pay bills and we purchase goods by entering our personal details all online and without a second thought. Have you ever stopped to wonder how much personal data you have shared online? Or what happens to that information? This new data protection regulation puts the consumer in the driver’s seat, and the task of complying with this regulation falls upon businesses and organizations. Otherwise, you're failing to comply, and as a result you can expect to face a hefty fine. GDPR’s impact is ten-fold on digital projects, providing a frame-work that must be followed strictly.
